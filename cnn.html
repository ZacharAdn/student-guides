🧠 What is a CNN?

A Convolutional Neural Network is a type of AI that learns to see. Just like your brain processes what your eyes see — a CNN processes images by breaking them down into simple patterns and building up to complex understanding.

👶

How a Baby Learns

A baby first learns to see edges and colors, then shapes, then faces, then recognizes "mom".

🤖

How a CNN Learns

A CNN first detects edges, then textures, then parts (ears, eyes), then recognizes "cat".

🔍

Like a Magnifying Glass

The CNN slides a small "window" across the image, looking for specific patterns at each position.

🧱

Building Blocks

Simple patterns combine into complex ones — edges → shapes → objects. Layer by layer.

✨ Key Takeaways

✅ Convolution = slide a small filter to detect patterns

✅ ReLU = keep positive values, zero out negatives

✅ Pooling = shrink the data, keep important features

✅ Dense layers = make the final decision (classify)

✅ Deep = powerful — more layers = detects more complex things

✅ Learns automatically — the filter values are learned from data, not hand-crafted!
