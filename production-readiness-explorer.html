Key Takeaways

1
Demo and Production are different sports. A demo only has to work once, on a happy path. Production has to keep working under failure, load, breaches, and boring Tuesday mornings.
2
Seven dimensions, not a checklist. Reliability, Observability, Security, Deployability, Scalability, Durability, Cost & Governance. Each one is a separate muscle — fix the weakest first.
3
You're already strong on Reliability and Security. Your soft spots before the World Cup are Observability (metrics/alerts/Sentry) and Cost governance. Those are what'll bite you at 2 AM.
4
Not everything must ship before launch. Use the toggles as a prioritisation tool: if a "what-if all done" score jump comes cheap, do it first. If expensive (Docker, full CI/CD), park it for post-launch.
5
Write the 5-line DR plan now. Backups exist, but "what we do when they're needed" does not. Five lines in a README beats a perfect plan you never write.
This is a map, not a menu. Use the toggles to simulate what-if scenarios before the World Cup. Next meeting we'll pick together what must ship before launch and what can wait.
